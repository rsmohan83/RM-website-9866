Human Capital


It refers to the stock of people posses different skills, availability, efficiency to produce various goods and services that helps to promote country GDP(Growth and Developmemt).


If large no. of skillful people add into existing productive population then, country's efficiency for the promotion of Growth and Development tends to rise and a country become a progressive nation where GDP growth constantly moves into upward direction.


Gross National Product


It is a sum total of factor income earn by normal or non-residents of company during an accounting year including net factors income from abroad.


Human Capital Formation


It is a process of addition of extra skill,efficiency,productive attitude in the existing population,is called Human Capital Formation.


Example»Suppose your IIT and AIIMS Institutes develop the skills and availabilities among the productive population (students) and further this people could add an existing working population which helps to increase the total stock of such kind of population.

Human Resources


It refers to the number of human being who posses different skills,availabilities at the point of time and contribute their availabilities for the value addition in the economy.


Types of Sector


  • Primary Sector
  • Secondary Sector
  • Tertiary Sector

Primary Sector


All the activities which are performing by exploiting the natural resource is called Primary Sector.For example»Farm ing,Fishing,Mining.


Secondary Sector


All the activities which are engaged for converting the raw material into finished goods.For example»Industry,Manufacturing, Construction of building,etc.


Tertiary Sector


All the activities which are engaged for producing services is called Tertiary Sector.For example»Transportation,Communication,Shipping,Banking.


Market activities


The activities which are undertaken to manage the source of livelihood in terms of remuneration receive for services.


Non-Market activities


All the activities which are undertaken to manage self-owned consumption not for the market is called Non-Market activities. For example»A farmer produces food grains for self-consumption not for the marketable surplus.


Unemployment


It refers to situation in which a person who is willing to work but doesn't get a job at the existing market wage rate.


Seasonal Unemployment


It refers to situation in which a person would term to an unemployed due to change of season.


Disguised Unemployment


It refers to situation in which the number of people working more than the actual requirement.


Poverty Line


A line which bifurcates the group of people as poor or non-poor on the basis of income or consumption then,it is known as Poverty Line.


Absolute Poor


When a person failed to earn stimulated income fixed by the government then,a person would be considered as Absolute poor.


Relative Poor


When an individual's income or consumption is lower than his/her next high sub-ordinates then,the same would be considered as Relative Poor.


Food Security


Food Security means availability,affordability,accessibility of food to all people at all times.
